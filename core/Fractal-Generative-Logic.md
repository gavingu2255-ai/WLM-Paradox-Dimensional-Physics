# Fractal Generative Logic (FGL)
*A Foundational Framework for the 0‑Structure Generative Model*  
Version 1.0  
Author: Gavin Gu

---

## 1. Overview

**Fractal Generative Logic (FGL)** is the core generative mechanism of WLM’s **0‑Structure Model**.  
It describes how all structure, complexity, and world‑level phenomena emerge from a single invariant origin — **0** — through:

- **Offset**
- **Polarity**
- **Recursion**
- **Self‑similar structure**
- **Scale‑dependent manifestation**

FGL is not classical recursion, not mere iteration, and not just a metaphor.  
It is a **generative logic**: a way in which structure *necessarily appears* once the unmoving origin is minimally disturbed.

---

## 2. Core generative sequence

The essence of FGL can be stated as:

> **0 does not move.  
> Offset creates polarity.  
> Polarity recurses.  
> Recursion generates structure.  
> Structure expands.  
> The world appears.**

This sequence is not a narrative; it is a **structural law**.  
Once 0 is offset, the rest is not optional — it is **forced**.

---

## 3. The role of 0

In FGL, **0** is not a number. It is:

- A **non‑moving origin**
- A **carrier of all potential structure**
- A **reference that never participates in change**

Key properties:

1. **Invariance:** 0 never changes state.  
2. **Non‑participation:** 0 does not “enter” the world; the world appears as a consequence of offset *from* 0.  
3. **Reference:** all structure is intelligible only relative to 0.

FGL is therefore a **0‑anchored generative logic**.

---

## 4. Offset and polarity

The first event in FGL is **offset**:

- An offset is a minimal deviation from 0.
- This deviation is not yet “something”; it is the **possibility of difference**.

Once offset occurs, **polarity** appears:

- + / −  
- inside / outside  
- self / other  

Polarity is the first **structural distinction**.  
It is the seed of all later complexity.

In FGL:

- **Offset** = the act of deviating from 0  
- **Polarity** = the structural consequence of that deviation

---

## 5. Recursion and self‑similarity

Polarity does not remain static. It **recurses**:

- Polarity applies to itself.
- Distinctions generate further distinctions.
- The pattern of “difference” repeats at new scales.

This produces **self‑similarity**:

- The same structural pattern appears at multiple levels.
- Local configurations echo global configurations.
- The system becomes **fractal** in its organization.

This is why FGL is called **Fractal Generative Logic**:

- It is **fractal** because of self‑similarity across scales.
- It is **generative** because complexity emerges from a single origin.
- It is **logic** because the sequence is structurally necessary, not arbitrary.

---

## 6. From structure to world

As recursion continues, structure stabilizes:

1. **Primitive distinctions** → basic structural units  
2. **Relations between units** → patterns and networks  
3. **Stabilized patterns** → systems  
4. **Interacting systems** → what we call “worlds”

In FGL terms:

- The **world** is not a given.
- The world is the **visible consequence** of recursive structure generated from offset 0.

Thus:

> The world is a **fractal manifestation** of 0 under offset.

---

## 7. 0–36 as a fractal generative ladder

In WLM, the range **0–36** is not a numeric scale but a **structural ladder**:

- **0**: unmoving origin  
- **1–9**: structure generation layer (distinction, boundary, subject, world, closure)  
- **10–27**: world domain (language, systems, society, self‑correction, world‑closure)  
- **28–36**: execution domain (world‑level action, multi‑world interfaces, structural elevation)

Each level is:

- Not derived from the previous by simple recursion  
- But a **scale‑dependent manifestation of 0 under offset**

In other words:

> All levels are different **faces of the same origin**, seen at different structural scales.

---

## 8. Not recursion, but offset‑based fractal generation

It is tempting to call FGL “recursive,” but this is incomplete.

Classical recursion:

- f(n+1) is defined in terms of f(n)

FGL:

- f(n) is not derived from f(n−1)  
- f(n) is a **manifestation of 0 under a specific scale and offset**

So FGL is better described as:

> **Offset‑Based Fractal Generative Logic**

Key differences from recursion:

- No linear dependency chain (n → n+1)  
- All levels are anchored in 0, not in each other  
- Self‑similarity is structural, not procedural

## 9. Formal characterization (informal)

We can sketch FGL as:

- Let **0** be an invariant origin.  
- Let **Δ** be a minimal offset from 0.  
- Let **P(Δ)** be the induced polarity.  
- Let **R(P)** be the recursive application of polarity to itself.  
- Let **S(R)** be the emergent structure.  
- Let **W(S)** be the world‑level manifestation of structure.

Then:

- **FGL(0)** = W(S(R(P(Δ(0)))))  

But crucially:

- 0 remains unchanged throughout.  
- All complexity is a function of **offset**, not of 0’s internal change.

are all derived.

## 11. Summary

**Fractal Generative Logic (FGL)** states that:

- There is an unmoving origin: **0**.  
- A minimal **offset** from 0 creates **polarity**.  
- Polarity **recurses**, generating **self‑similar structure**.  
- Structure **expands across scales**, forming **worlds**.  
- All levels of structure are **scale‑dependent manifestations of 0 under offset**.

FGL is therefore:

- A **0‑anchored generative logic**  
- A **fractal model of structure and world**  
- The **foundational layer** of WLM’s 0‑Structure Generative Model

It does not describe *what* the world is.  
It describes **how any world can appear at all.**
