## Why 0–36 Forms a Complete Structural Loop

The range **0–36** represents the full, closed cycle of world‑level structure.  
It begins with the manifest appearance of the origin (0–18),  
passes through the first complete subject–world recursion (19–27),  
and stabilizes as a fully autonomous world‑scale system (27–36).

These three arcs together form a **self‑contained structural loop**:

- **0–18** — the manifest layer of Zero  
  (the origin as it becomes visible inside a world)

- **19–27** — the first full recursion  
  (the subject and world completing their mutual closure)

- **27–36** — the stabilization arc  
  (the world achieving autonomy as a coherent structure)

Once 36 is reached, the world‑level cycle is complete.  
Nothing essential to world formation lies outside this range.

> **0–36 is the entire “world‑as‑container” loop.  
> Beyond 36, the container flips.**

Everything after 36 belongs to the next recursion layer,  
where the world is no longer the container but becomes the object.  
For this reason, **0–36 is published in full**,  
while 36+ remains intentionally non‑public.
